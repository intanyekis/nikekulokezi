Gloria Molina Career

Molina's early career was marked by her participation in the Chicano movement and advocacy for women's health. As a teenage participant in the Chicano movement, Molina entered politics in 1982, when she ran against the strong male-dominated Eastside political machine for a seat in the California State Assembly, becoming the first Latina elected to the Assembly. She worked as a deputy director for the Department of Health and Human Services in San Francisco. Molina investigated a program called the Gloria Molina Foster Youth Education Programme in 2008. Molina indicated in 2014 that she will challenge 14th district incumbent José Huizar for his position on the City Council, citing a term restriction on the Board of Supervisors. Following the period on the board, Molina campaigned for the Los Angeles City Council again in 2015 but was defeated by incumbent José Huizar.

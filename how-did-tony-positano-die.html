Who Was Tony Positano?

Tony Positano was born in Courtice, Ontario, Canada. From a young age, he had a deep passion for automobiles and speed. As he grew older, his love for racing cars led him to the world of drag racing, where he found his true calling. Positano quickly gained recognition for his exceptional skills behind the wheel, becoming a prominent figure in the Canadian drag racing scene.

Tony got the racing appetite in the early ’70s while helping his best friend race his ’69 Nova. Then he moved into a 1941 Willy’s (Chilly Willy) that was once owned by Burt Straus.

In 1990, he and a friend purchased a 1970 Chevelle in California, and they raced it for two years. In 1992, he bought a 1977 Vega Wagon with a small block in it. He raced it at ST. Thomas every weekend from 1992 to 1996, clocking 9.50s.

Throughout his career, Tony Positano achieved numerous milestones and accolades, earning respect and admiration from his peers and fans alike. His dedication to the sport was unmatched, and he continuously pushed the boundaries of what was possible in drag racing. He participated in various racing events, including the National Hot Rod Association (NHRA) events, where he made a name for himself as a fierce and determined competitor.

Positano’s career was not without its fair share of challenges and setbacks. Like any racer, he faced the highs and lows of the sport, battling mechanical failures, tough competition, and the constant pursuit of perfection. However, he persevered through it all, leaving a lasting legacy in the Canadian racing community.

How Did Tony Positano Die? Cause of Death Explained

Tony Positano, a well-known Top Dragster Rival from Courtice, Ontario, tragically passed away on Monday, September 3rd, 2023, leaving those who knew him in utter shock. He was known for his fearless and competitive spirit, and who had a long and illustrious career in the world of Canadian drag racing.

On the fateful day of Monday, tragedy struck the drag racing world as news of Tony Positano’s passing spread like wildfire.

According to various reports, the beloved racer had been involved in a fatal accident during a drag racing event in Cayuga, Ontario. Details surrounding the accident are still emerging, and the investigation is ongoing.

DragRaceCanada shared the news on Facebook and penned a heartfelt note for him, saying “We mourn the untimely passing of well-liked and devoted Top Dragster class racer Tony Positano from Courtice, Ontario, along with all of Canadian drag racing.  A high-speed incident at Cayuga Dragway earlier today claimed Tony’s life.”

Tributes pour for Tony Positano

Since the news of Tony Positano’s demise came into existence, many of his friends, family, and loved ones took to various social media accounts to express their disbelief and to pay tribute to him.

Heather Kovacs Antinora posted on Facebook, saying “The family and team of fellow top dragster driver Tony Positano have Antinora Family Racing’s sincere condolences. Tony and I recently competed together, and we were fortunate to have the chance to catch up while seated in the staging lanes. May you rest in peace forever. Goodbye, my beloved friend.”

In a post, MaryAnn Williams mentioned, “You sometimes come across a particular person in your life who is always smiling and enjoying life. Apart from his family, Tony enjoyed racing the most, but today, in a horrible accident, that life was gone much too quickly. Without Tony and that enormous cigar he used to smoke, the race track wouldn’t be the same. Good luck, Tony! My friend, you will be missed.”

Race Cayuga Sportsman Series stated, “A great gentleman, Tony Positano loved racing and was a part of numerous racing families. Tony’s family is in our thoughts and prayers during this trying time. He always worked to develop the sport and encourage more people to participate in racing.”

The passing of Tony Positano, a Canadian drag racing icon, is a profound loss to the racing community. His life and career were marked by passion, dedication, and a solid strive for excellence.  Tony Positano’s legacy will continue to inspire racers and fans alike, reminding us all of the joy and camaraderie that makes drag racing such a unique and cherished sport.
